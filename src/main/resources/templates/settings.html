Risk Controls
Enable structural SL
ATR Period Buffer × ATR
Single-level extra buffer × ATR (R2/R3/R4, S2/S3/S4 only)
Apply single-level buffer to zone setups when CPR width < % (0 = disabled)
SL = anchor ± buffer × ATR(period). Anchored to the broken level (CPR, R1/PDH, R2, etc.). The single-level extra buffer is added only to setups that lack a zone-width cushion (R2/R3/R4, S2/S3/S4); zone setups (CPR, R1+PDH, S1+PDL, magnets) get their cushion from the zone width itself unless CPR width % is below the threshold above — then the zone is too tight to absorb a normal pullback, so the extra buffer is added to those zone setups too. Breakout detection still uses the full zone. ATR period also used for candle filters and volatility measures.
Skip trade if reward/risk < :1
Computed after SL and final target are determined. R/R = |target − entry| / |entry − SL|. Uses the final target (T2 for split trades) and the final SL (structural or default, whichever was picked). Default 1.0 means reject any trade where reward is less than risk.
Enable breakeven stop-loss
Trigger @ % of range → SL at entry ± × ATR
Range = entry (0%) → target (100%). When peak/trough reaches the trigger %, SL moves to entry ± buffer × ATR (locks in a small profit, never widens). For split-target trades, breakeven is suppressed until T1 fills, then activates on the remaining qty. Defaults: 61.8 / 1.
Stock Price vs EMA 20 — exit when stock's 5-min candle close is on the wrong side of its EMA 20 (LONG: close < EMA 20; SHORT: close > EMA 20)
Stock-side defensive-exit check, evaluated on every 5-min candle close (after the candle's EMA is stamped). Fires before SL / Target / Breakeven; cancels both OCO legs and places an exit market order. Reason recorded: PRICE_EMA_EXIT. NIFTY-side macro safety-net mirrors of this check live on the Index Alignment tab.
Trade Filters
Price: 5-min EMA trend — close above EMA 20 for buys, close below EMA 20 for sells
Gates entries by price position relative to the 5-min EMA 20. Buys require close > EMA 20; sells require close < EMA 20. Failing the gate rejects the trade. Fail-open when EMA 20 is still warming up.
Buy only above ATP (VWAP), sell only below ATP
Buy only when 20 EMA > ATP, sell only when 20 EMA < ATP
ATP is the exchange's VWAP — today's volume-weighted average price. Resets daily, independent of EMAs (which span days). Captures intraday sentiment: price above ATP = bulls in control today, below = bears. Complements the EMA filter above.
Skip if any CPR zone sits between EMA and the broken level EMA must sit within % (away from boundary = close to broken level)
Bypass this filter until (IST — momentum often outruns EMA in the first hour)
Zone count check. Counts CPR zones (S4, S3, S2, S1/PDL, CPR, R1/PDH, R2, R3, R4) strictly between the 20 EMA and the breakout level. Pair zones (CPR, R1/PDH, S1/PDL) count as one. The breakout zone itself is excluded. If 0 zones in between, the trade fires; if 1 or more, the setup is stale and skipped.

Proximity check (default 50%). The setting reads as "EMA must sit within N% away from the boundary edge" — equivalently, within the closest (100−N)% of the range from the broken level. The 100% range is measured from the broken level to the next-zone edge. Higher N = stricter (EMA must be closer to broken).
Example: BUY_ABOVE_R1_PDH with broken=R1+PDH=100, next zone above (R2)=105. Range = 5. At N=75, EMA must sit within (100−75)% = 25% of range from broken → EMA ≥ 98.75 (close to broken). EMA at 96 = 80% of range away from broken → skipped (too far). Set to 0 to disable proximity check (use zone-count only).
Skip R3/S3 breakouts (and DH above R3 / DL below S3) — IV / OV days
Skip R3/S3 breakouts (and DH above R3 / DL below S3) — EV days
Skip R4/S4 breakouts (and DH above R4 / DL below S4) — IV / OV days
Skip R4/S4 breakouts (and DH above R4 / DL below S4) — EV days
Day type is set by the open print (first 5-min close) vs daily CPR + R2/S2:
• IV — open inside CPR (between BC and TC)
• OV — open between CPR and R2/S2
• EV — open outside R2/S2 (extreme-value gap)
Extended-level breakouts (R3/S3, R4/S4) are far from the daily CPR and historically unreliable on regular days. EV days are different — an R3 break following an open above R2 is fresh gap momentum, not exhaustion. Toggle the IV/OV and EV gates independently to match your strategy. R2/S2 always fires regardless. No per-level qty reduction.

Candle Pattern Thresholds
Tunable thresholds for the bullish/bearish candle patterns used by the CPR retest entries: hammer / shooting star, outside reversal (engulfing), doji reversal, morning / evening star, harami, and good-size candle (catch-all). All share the retest-only model: the level must already be broken (state captured by the level-broken state machine) and the current bar must touch the level and close back in trade direction. Use higher numbers to make a pattern stricter (fewer signals, higher quality); lower numbers to loosen it.
Bar 1 and Bar 3 body in [ , ] × ATR Bar 2 body ≤ × bar 1 body
Bar 3 penetrates ≥ into bar 1 body (0.5 = 50%)
Bars 1 and 3 share the same [floor, ceiling] × ATR band — by classical convention (Nison) the outer bars are treated as symmetric impulses. Bar 2 (the "star") stays small relative to bar 1. Bar 3 penetration sets how deep into bar 1's body bar 3 must close: 0.0 = past bar 1's close (any recovery), 0.5 = past midpoint (Nison default), 1.0 = past bar 1's open (full reclaim, same as Three Inside Up). Defaults: 0.5–2.0 × ATR outer bars, 0.3 × bar1 body for the middle bar, 0.5 penetration. Set the ceiling to 0 to disable the size cap.
Bar 1 and Bar 3 body in [ , ] × ATR
Bar 3 penetrates ≥ into bar 1 body (1.0 = past bar 1 open)
Bars 1 and 3 share the same [floor, ceiling] × ATR band — symmetric with Morning/Evening Star. Floor keeps both outer bars meaningful; ceiling skips climactic exhaustion. Bar 2 opposite-color, body fully INSIDE bar 1's body — classical Harami containment, no extra size cap. Bar 3 penetration: 1.0 = past bar 1's open (classical Nison Three Inside Up/Down), 0.5 = past midpoint, 0.0 = past bar 1's close (any recovery). Defaults: 0.5–2.0 × ATR shared band, 1.0 penetration. Set any side to 0 to disable.
Bar 1 and Bar 2 body in [ , ] × ATR
Bar 2 penetrates ≥ into bar 1 body (1.0 = classical engulfing — bar 2 closes past bar 1's open)
Classical 2-bar bullish/bearish engulfing. Strict color flip required (red→green for bullish, green→red for bearish). Bars 1 and 2 share the same [floor, ceiling] × ATR band. Bar 2 penetration: 1.0 = past bar 1's open (classical strict engulfing, default); 0.5 = past midpoint (accepts shallower reversals); 0.0 = past bar 1's close (any directional recovery). Defaults: 0.5–2.0 × ATR shared band, 1.0 penetration. Trade-log tag: OUTSIDE_REVERSAL_RETEST.
Doji body ≤ × range Confirm body in [ , ] × ATR
Bar 1 is the doji (tiny body relative to its range). Bar 2 is the directional confirmation — body must sit inside the band [floor, ceiling] × ATR AND close past the doji's entire range: bullish reversal closes past doji's high, bearish closes past doji's low. The close-past-range rule is hardcoded (not configurable) — it ensures the reversal bar fully eclipses the indecision bar. Defaults: 0.10 × range (10%) for the doji, 0.5–2.0 × ATR for the confirmation. Set the ceiling to 0 to disable the cap.
Rejection wick ≥ × body Opposite wick ≤ × body
Hammer (buy) needs a long lower wick + small upper wick. Shooting star (sell) is the mirror. Defaults: 2.0 × body rejection wick, 0.30 × body opposite wick.
Body in [ , ] × ATR
Last in the matcher chain — fires only when no stricter named pattern matched. Body must sit inside the [floor, ceiling] × ATR band — floor keeps weak bars out, ceiling kills oversized exhaustion / blow-off bars. The opposing-wick cap is controlled by the shared Confirmation Max Opposing Wick setting at the top of this section. Defaults: 0.6–2.0 × ATR body.
Slack ≤ × ATR
A retest pattern's bar can fall short of the level by up to this much ATR and still count as a touch. Absorbs tick-level whisker misses. Applies to all 6 retest patterns (hammer, outside-reversal, doji, star, harami, good-size). Set to 0 to require strict equality. Default: 0.2 × ATR.
Opposing wick ≤ × body
Applied to the confirmation bar of every pattern — bar 2 of Outside Reversal / Doji Reversal, bar 3 of Morning/Evening Star and Three Inside Up/Down, and the single-bar Good Size catch-all. For bullish patterns this caps the upper wick; for bearish, the lower wick. Default 0.5 (wick can be at most half the body). Set to 0 to disable entirely. Rejects bars whose body says "buyer/seller commitment" but whose wick says "got rejected at the extreme."
NIFTY Alignment Filters
Enable NIFTY trend alignment filter
Include NIFTY 20 EMA in trend identification
Include NIFTY FUT VWAP in trend identification
NIFTY trend state combines CPR + Futures VWAP (always) plus optionally EMA 20:
  • CPR: NIFTY index's last-completed 5-min close above (bullish) or below (bearish) daily CPR. Inside CPR leaves this factor null.
  • Futures VWAP: NIFTY futures' last-completed 5-min close vs that same bar's stamped VWAP (Fyers ATP frozen at finalize). Close > VWAP = bullish; close < VWAP = bearish. Same-bar coherent — no mixing of live LTP with frozen VWAP.
  • EMA 20 (optional): NIFTY's last-completed 5-min close vs its 20-period 5-min EMA. When enabled, this becomes a third confirmation gating the BULLISH REVERSAL / BEARISH REVERSAL states — the reversal only fires when EMA and futures VWAP both agree. When disabled, the reversal states rely purely on CPR-disagrees-with-futVwap (the original behavior).
States:
  • BULLISH — CPR bullish + futures vs VWAP bullish (clear trend agreement)
  • BEARISH — CPR bearish + futures vs VWAP bearish (mirror)
  • BULLISH REVERSAL — CPR bearish but futures vs VWAP flipped bullish (institutions buying despite spot below CPR — downtrend rolling over)
  • BEARISH REVERSAL — CPR bullish but futures vs VWAP flipped bearish (institutions selling despite spot above CPR — uptrend rolling over)
  • SIDEWAYS — one factor determined but the other null (inside CPR with futures determined, or vice versa)
  • NEUTRAL — both factors null (pre-market / no completed 5-min bar yet)
When this filter is on: buys require BULLISH or BULLISH REVERSAL; sells require BEARISH or BEARISH REVERSAL. SIDEWAYS / NEUTRAL / opposite-direction states hard-reject the signal as NIFTY_OPPOSED. Independent of the other NIFTY filters below; all can be enabled together.
Reject ALL stock trades when NIFTY's 15-min close hasn't cleared its nearest weekly-levels hurdle
Min headroom to next hurdle: × NIFTY ATR (0 = off)
Macro-level mirror of the per-stock HTF Hurdle, applied to NIFTY's own data. Identifies NIFTY's nearest weekly-levels hurdle in trade direction and rejects every stock signal while NIFTY's most-recently-completed 15-min close hasn't decisively cleared it. The 15-min boundary aligns to wall-clock quarters (9:30, 9:45, 10:00…); at a 15-min boundary stock bar this IS the just-closed 15-min bar (same-candle, no artificial lag), and between boundaries it's the latest 15-min that has closed. Pre-9:30 IST no 15-min close exists yet so the filter silently fail-opens until the first one finalizes at 9:30.
  • Buys — nearest of {weekly R1, PWH, weekly TC, weekly Pivot, weekly BC} below NIFTY LTP
  • Sells — nearest of {weekly S1, PWL, weekly TC, weekly Pivot, weekly BC} above NIFTY LTP
Max Call/Put OI is a separate filter (see below) so it can be toggled independently.

Headroom check. Even after the past hurdle is cleared, the filter also checks the nearest hurdle in the opposite direction (above NIFTY LTP for buys / below for sells). If that upcoming hurdle is closer than min headroom × NIFTY ATR, the trade is rejected. Default 1.0 × ATR (5-min ATR, matches the scanner timeframe so headroom reads against the bar size the bot actually trades on). Set to 0 to disable just the headroom check. Signal Trail bucket: NIFTY_HURDLE.
Reject ALL stock trades when NIFTY's 5m close is inside / hasn't fully cleared its nearest CPR / R1+PDH / S1+PDL zone
Min headroom to next hurdle: × NIFTY ATR (0 = off)
5-min variant of the NIFTY HTF Hurdle, against NIFTY's daily levels treated as zones: CPR [min(TC,BC), max(TC,BC)], R1+PDH [min(R1,PH), max(R1,PH)] and S1+PDL [min(S1,PL), max(S1,PL)]. CPR is included — redundant with Index Alignment for non-reversal aligned trades, but load-bearing for reversal states (BULLISH_REVERSAL with NIFTY below CPR, BEARISH_REVERSAL with NIFTY above CPR) where CPR sits ahead of LTP in the trade direction. Extended levels (R2/R3/R4, S2/S3/S4) excluded. Picks the behind zone in trade direction (highest zone whose lower edge is at/below NIFTY LTP for buys; lowest zone whose upper edge is at/above LTP for sells) and rejects if NIFTY's current same-bucket 5m close is inside that zone or short of its far edge — clearing only the inner line of a zone isn't enough; the close must clear the far edge. Fail-open if NIFTY's same-bucket bar isn't yet completed.

Headroom check. Even after the behind zone is fully cleared, the filter rejects if the next zone ahead (lower edge above LTP for buys / upper edge below LTP for sells) is closer than min headroom × NIFTY ATR. Default 1.5 × ATR. Set to 0 to disable just the headroom check. Signal Trail bucket: NIFTY_5M_HURDLE.
Reject ALL stock trades when NIFTY is inside or near the active virgin CPR zone
Min headroom to zone edge: × NIFTY ATR (0 = off)
Treats the active virgin CPR as a zone (BC..TC), not individual levels. Uses NIFTY's current same-bucket 5m close (the bar that just fired the stock signal) — no one-bar lag.
  • Inside zone — NIFTY's 5m close lands within [BC, TC]: every stock signal rejected, both directions.
  • Headroom — close outside the zone but within headroom × NIFTY ATR of the zone edge in the trade direction: rejected. Buys blocked when zone is overhead and close; sells blocked when zone is underfoot and close. Mirrors the 5m hurdle's directional headroom logic.
Default headroom 1.0 × ATR. Set to 0 to disable the headroom leg (the inside-zone leg stays on while the master toggle is on). Signal Trail bucket: VIRGIN_CPR_HURDLE.
Per-Stock HTF Alignment Filters
Reject when prior 15-min close hasn't cleared nearest weekly R1/PWH/TC/Pivot/BC (buy) or S1/PWL/TC/Pivot/BC (sell)
For a 5-min breakout, find the nearest weekly hurdle in the trade direction: buys → the highest of {R1, PWH, weekly TC, weekly Pivot, weekly BC} strictly below the 5-min close; sells → the lowest of {S1, PWL, weekly TC, weekly Pivot, weekly BC} strictly above the close. Then check the previous 15-min close (mirrors the NIFTY HTF Hurdle): cleared → trade fires; didn't clear → reject; no prior 15-min close yet today (pre-9:30 IST) → silent fail-open. Weekly CPR (TC/Pivot/BC) is included so that breakouts that fired just past the weekly CPR boundary are gated by the prior 15-min close too — e.g. a buy at R1 in a stock currently inside weekly CPR but BULLISH by the state machine. R2+/S2+ are excluded — far-out projections, not walls the HTF needs to clear first. Applies to all HPT and MPT breakouts. Fail-open when any level is warming up.
Buy needs in-progress 1h candle green; sell needs red
Per-stock candle-direction confluence check. When on, every 5-min breakout must agree with the currently-forming 60-min HTF candle's body direction:
  • Buy — 1h close > 1h open (green). Reject if 1h is strictly red.
  • Sell — 1h close < 1h open (red). Reject if 1h is strictly green.
Doji (close == open) passes both directions. No in-progress 1h bar yet (first ticks of a new 1h bucket) → fail-open. Independent of HTF Hurdle / HTF EMA filters — this looks at the currently-forming bar's intra-bar direction, not the prior close. Signal Trail bucket: HTF_CANDLE_OPPOSED.
Skip any breakout with entry close beyond weekly R3 (buys) or weekly S3 (sells)
Skip any breakout with entry close beyond weekly R4 (buys) or weekly S4 (sells)
Independent of the daily extended-level skips on the Filters tab. Trades whose entry sits past the weekly extended levels carry the same exhaustion concern as a daily-R3 trade — even if the daily setup is just an R1 break. Enable both for the strictest gating; either toggle independently otherwise. Fail-open if weekly levels haven't loaded yet.
Target Settings
Shift target to weekly CPR level when one sits between entry and chosen daily target
The target picker walks the daily CPR ladder (S4 ... R4 plus mid-points) closest-first until a level satisfies min R/R. With this on, the picker then checks all weekly CPR levels (Pivot, TC, BC, R1-R4, S1-S4, PWH, PWL) — if one sits strictly between entry and the chosen daily, the target is shifted to the weekly closest to entry and R/R is re-checked. If the shifted R/R falls below the minimum, the trade is rejected. With this off, only daily CPR levels are considered — whatever the daily walk picks becomes the final target as-is.
Discount target by ATR (places target inside the level)
Markets often reverse just before touching CPR levels. The target order is placed slightly inside the level so near-miss reversals still fill.
₹
Skip trade if expected profit (qty × target distance) is below this amount. Catches trades where stacked qty reductions made the position too small. Set to 0 to disable.
