Risk Controls
Enable structural SL
ATR Period Buffer × ATR
Single-level extra buffer × ATR (R2/R3/R4, S2/S3/S4, DH/DL only)
SL = anchor ± buffer × ATR(period). Anchored to the broken level (CPR, R1/PDH, R2, etc.) or candle wick (for DH/DL). The single-level extra buffer is added only to setups that lack a zone-width cushion (R2/R3/R4, S2/S3/S4, DH/DL); zone setups (CPR, R1+PDH, S1+PDL, magnets) get their cushion from the zone width itself. ATR period also used for candle filters and volatility measures.
Skip trade if reward/risk < :1
Computed after SL and final target are determined. R/R = |target − entry| / |entry − SL|. Uses the final target (T2 for split trades) and the final SL (structural or default, whichever was picked). Default 1.0 means reject any trade where reward is less than risk.
Enable Fibonacci trailing stop-loss
Stage 1 @ % Fib → SL at entry ± × ATR
Stage 2 @ % Fib → SL at % Fib of range
Range = breakout level (0%) → target (100%). SL only tightens, never widens. For split-target trades, trailing is suppressed until T1 fills; after T1, Fibonacci activates on the remaining qty using the same thresholds. Defaults: 61.8 / 1 / 78.6 / 61.8.
Stop trading a symbol once today's wins or losses reach (0 = disabled)
Wins and losses are counted separately per symbol per trading day, not summed. With limit = 2: 2W + 0L → stop, 0W + 2L → stop, 1W + 1L → continue. T1 partial fills are not counted — a split trade that hits both T1 and T2 counts as one win. Counter resets daily. Skipped signals appear in Signal Trail under bucket SYMBOL_DAILY_LIMIT.
Enable LPT — fire NIFTY-misaligned breakouts at reduced size instead of rejecting them
LPT Qty × (0.5 = half size)
Max LPT trades per stock per day (0 = disabled)
When a stock breakout passes all stock-level filters but is misaligned with the NIFTY Index Alignment filter, the trade is downgraded to LPT instead of rejected. LPT trades skip remaining NIFTY-level filters (HTF Hurdle, 5m Hurdle, NIFTY HTF Candle Direction) and fire at LPT Qty × base qty (default 0.5). Toggle off to revert to outright rejection. The daily cap (default 1) limits LPT count per stock — over the cap, signals reject under bucket LPT_DAILY_LIMIT. T1 partial fills are not counted.
Stock SMA Cross — exit when stock's 5-min SMA 20 stacks against trade direction (LONG: 20 < 50; SHORT: 20 > 50)
Stock Price vs SMA 50 — exit when stock's 5-min candle close is on the wrong side of its SMA 50 (LONG: close < SMA 50; SHORT: close > SMA 50)
Stock-side defensive-exit checks, evaluated on every 5-min candle close (after the candle's SMAs are stamped). Fire before SL / Target / Trailing; cancels both OCO legs and places an exit market order. Reasons recorded: SMA_CROSS_EXIT, PRICE_SMA_EXIT. Checks run in order; first match wins. NIFTY-side macro safety-net mirrors of these checks live on the Index Alignment tab.
Exit when NIFTY's reversal move touches its CPR boundary (early profit-take)
Macro defensive exit for trades taken in NIFTY reversal states. Evaluated on every stock 5-min candle close.
  • BULLISH_REVERSAL — NIFTY below CPR + bullish SMAs. We took LONG positions on stocks. When NIFTY climbs back to/past CPR bottom, the bullish move has played out (NIFTY entering CPR consolidation) → exit all LONG positions.
  • BEARISH_REVERSAL — NIFTY above CPR + bearish SMAs. We took SHORT positions. When NIFTY drops to/below CPR top → exit all SHORT positions.
Locks in the move's profit before NIFTY consolidates inside CPR and the trade goes against us. Cancels both OCO legs and places a market exit. Reason recorded: NIFTY_REVERSAL_CPR_EXIT.
Trade Filters
Price (lenient): 5-min SMA trend — close above/below SMA 20 and 50 (skip 200)
Price (strict): 5-min SMA trend — close above/below SMA 20, 50, 200 together
Alignment (lenient): SMAs aligned — buy requires 20 > 50 only, sell requires 20 < 50 only (skip 200)
Alignment (strict): SMAs aligned — buy requires 20 > 50 > 200, sell requires 20 < 50 < 200
Pattern: require direction-matched 5-min pattern — R-RTP for buys, F-RTP for sells
Pattern: skip trades when 5-min pattern is ZIG ZAG (choppy)
Two dimensions gate an entry: price position (close vs SMAs) and alignment (SMA-to-SMA ordering). Each has a lenient (uses 20 + 50 only) and a strict (uses 20 + 50 + 200) variant — strict implies lenient, so when both are on the strict gate wins.

Price (lenient): close above/below 20 and 50. Price (strict): close above/below 20, 50, 200 together.
Alignment (lenient): 20 > 50 (buy) / 20 < 50 (sell). Alignment (strict): 20 > 50 > 200 (buy) / 20 < 50 < 200 (sell).

Failing any enabled gate rejects the trade (no qty downgrade under the LTF-priority model). Fail-open when any SMA is still warming up. When both price gates are OFF, the daily 200 SMA becomes a target-shift candidate (see Target Shift Sources).
Buy only above ATP (VWAP), sell only below ATP
Buy only when 20 SMA > ATP, sell only when 20 SMA < ATP
ATP is the exchange's VWAP — today's volume-weighted average price. Resets daily, independent of SMAs (which span days). Captures intraday sentiment: price above ATP = bulls in control today, below = bears. Complements the SMA filter above.
Reject if body < ATR AND move < ATR from breakout level
OR-logic: candle passes if EITHER body or move past level meets its floor. Body floor = "meaningful conviction" (default 0.5). Move floor = "level actually cleared" (default 0.15 — small push past level is OK when body is strong).
Breakout wick ratio: wick/body to allow small body (default 1.5)
Opposite wick ratio: wick/body to reject counter-pressure (default 2.0)
Skip if candle body > × ATR
Rejects breakout candles with excessively large body (|close − open|). Catches exhaustion moves where the stock has already run too far on a single candle. Default 4.0 ATR catches only extreme spikes.
Skip if any CPR zone sits between SMA and the broken level SMA must sit within % (away from boundary = close to broken level)
Bypass this filter until (IST — momentum often outruns SMA in the first hour)
Zone count check. Counts CPR zones (S4, S3, S2, S1/PDL, CPR, R1/PDH, R2, R3, R4) strictly between the 20 SMA and the breakout level. Pair zones (CPR, R1/PDH, S1/PDL) count as one. The breakout zone itself is excluded — for DH/DL setups no zone is excluded since DH/DL aren't CPR-defined. If 0 zones in between, the trade fires; if 1 or more, the setup is stale and skipped.

Proximity check (default 50%). The setting reads as "SMA must sit within N% away from the boundary edge" — equivalently, within the closest (100−N)% of the range from the broken level. The 100% range is measured from the broken level to the next-zone edge. Higher N = stricter (SMA must be closer to broken).
Example: BUY_ABOVE_R1_PDH with broken=R1+PDH=100, next zone above (R2)=105. Range = 5. At N=75, SMA must sit within (100−75)% = 25% of range from broken → SMA ≥ 98.75 (close to broken). SMA at 96 = 80% of range away from broken → skipped (too far). Set to 0 to disable proximity check (use zone-count only).
Skip R3/S3 breakouts (and DH above R3 / DL below S3) — IV / OV days
Skip R3/S3 breakouts (and DH above R3 / DL below S3) — EV days
Skip R4/S4 breakouts (and DH above R4 / DL below S4) — IV / OV days
Skip R4/S4 breakouts (and DH above R4 / DL below S4) — EV days
Day type is set by the open print (first 5-min close) vs daily CPR + R2/S2:
• IV — open inside CPR (between BC and TC)
• OV — open between CPR and R2/S2
• EV — open outside R2/S2 (extreme-value gap)
Extended-level breakouts (R3/S3, R4/S4) are far from the daily CPR and historically unreliable on regular days. EV days are different — an R3 break following an open above R2 is fresh gap momentum, not exhaustion. Toggle the IV/OV and EV gates independently to match your strategy. DH/DL breakouts that have already passed the level are treated identically. R2/S2 always fires regardless. No per-level qty reduction.
Enable counter-trend setups (magnets + deep mean-reversion fades)
MPT Qty × (0.75 = three-quarter size)
Master switch for the counter-trend family. Setups in this family classify as MPT (medium probability) and fire at MPT Qty × base size (default 0.75).
  • Magnets — BUY_ABOVE_S1_PDL, SELL_BELOW_R1_PDH: bounce off / rejection at the first structural level pair, target back to CPR.
  • Deep mean-rev — BUY_ABOVE_S2/S3/S4, SELL_BELOW_R2/R3/R4: fades from deep support/resistance, target one level back toward value.
  • DH/DL breakouts — BUY_ABOVE_DH, SELL_BELOW_DL when the close is on the wrong side of CPR (otherwise they classify as trend-following HPT and skip this gate).
All counter-trend setups bypass the LTF gate by design. When the master toggle is off, none of them fire — only direction-aligned weekly-level breakouts (R1+/R2/R3/R4 buys, S1+/S2/S3/S4 sells) trade. Signal Trail bucket: MEAN_REVERSION_DISABLED.

NIFTY Alignment Filters
Enable NIFTY trend alignment filter
NIFTY trend state combines three sticky factors, all refreshed only at NIFTY's 5-min candle close:
  • CPR: NIFTY LTP above (bullish) or below (bearish) daily CPR
  • SMA Price: NIFTY LTP vs 5-min SMA 50, gated by SMA alignment. Bullish when SMA 20 > 50 AND price > SMA 50. Bearish when SMA 20 < 50 AND price < SMA 50. Trend stays intact through pullbacks toward SMA 20 — only a close through SMA 50 against the trend flips it to sideways.
  • SMA Alignment: 5-min SMA 20 > 50 (bullish) or 20 < 50 (bearish)
States:
  • BULLISH — CPR bullish + both SMA factors bullish; OR inside CPR + both SMA factors bullish (SMA structure drives the trend during NIFTY consolidation)
  • BEARISH — CPR bearish + both SMA factors bearish; OR inside CPR + both SMA factors bearish
  • BULLISH REVERSAL — CPR bearish but both SMA factors flipped bullish (downtrend rolling over)
  • BEARISH REVERSAL — CPR bullish but both SMA factors flipped bearish (uptrend rolling over)
  • SIDEWAYS — SMA factors mixed (whether CPR is determined or NIFTY is inside CPR)
  • NEUTRAL — LTP / CPR data missing, or SMAs not yet seeded
When this filter is on: buys require BULLISH or BULLISH REVERSAL; sells require BEARISH or BEARISH REVERSAL; SIDEWAYS / NEUTRAL / opposite-direction states skip the trade outright. Independent of the other NIFTY filters below; all can be enabled together.
Reject ALL stock trades when NIFTY's prior 1h close hasn't cleared its nearest weekly-levels hurdle
Min headroom to next hurdle: × NIFTY ATR (0 = off)
Macro-level mirror of the per-stock HTF Hurdle, applied to NIFTY's own data. Identifies NIFTY's nearest weekly-levels hurdle in trade direction and rejects every stock signal while NIFTY's prior 1h close hasn't decisively cleared it.
  • Buys — nearest of {weekly R1, PWH, weekly TC, weekly Pivot, weekly BC} below NIFTY LTP
  • Sells — nearest of {weekly S1, PWL, weekly TC, weekly Pivot, weekly BC} above NIFTY LTP
Max Call/Put OI is now a separate filter (see below) so it can be toggled independently.

Headroom check. Even after the past hurdle is cleared, the filter also checks the nearest hurdle in the opposite direction (above NIFTY LTP for buys / below for sells). If that upcoming hurdle is closer than min headroom × NIFTY ATR, the trade is rejected. Default 1.0 × ATR. Set to 0 to disable just the headroom check. Signal Trail bucket: NIFTY_HURDLE.
Reject ALL stock trades when NIFTY's prior 1h close hasn't cleared the Max Call/Put OI strike
Min headroom to OI strike: × NIFTY ATR (0 = off)
Same prior-1h-close + headroom logic as the HTF Hurdle, but checks only the Max Call OI strike (buys) or Max Put OI strike (sells). Refreshed every 15 minutes from NIFTY's option chain (Max Call OI = intraday resistance from option writers; Max Put OI = intraday support). Fail-open before the first OI fetch (no rejection without data). Signal Trail bucket: NIFTY_OI_HURDLE.
Reject ALL stock trades when NIFTY's prior 5m close hasn't cleared its nearest daily CPR hurdle
Min headroom to next hurdle: × NIFTY ATR (0 = off)
5-min variant of the NIFTY HTF Hurdle, against NIFTY's daily CPR inner zone. Candidate hurdles in trade direction: BC, Pivot, TC, R1, PDH for buys; TC, Pivot, BC, S1, PDL for sells — extended levels (R2/R3/R4, S2/S3/S4) intentionally excluded so the filter is about inner-zone confirmation, not far-out projections. Active virgin CPR levels are also appended when present. Rejects every stock signal while NIFTY's prior 5m close hasn't decisively cleared the nearest in-direction candidate. Guards against firing while NIFTY's current 5m bar is just now crossing a daily level — wait for the prior bar's close to confirm. Fail-open at session open (no prior 5m available).

Headroom check. Mirror of the NIFTY HTF Hurdle headroom — even after the past hurdle is cleared, the filter rejects if the nearest upcoming hurdle (above NIFTY LTP for buys / below for sells, from the same daily-CPR set) is closer than min headroom × NIFTY ATR. Default 1.0 × ATR. Set to 0 to disable just the headroom check. Signal Trail bucket: NIFTY_5M_HURDLE.
Reject ALL stock trades when NIFTY is inside or near the active virgin CPR zone
Min headroom to zone edge: × NIFTY ATR (0 = off)
Treats the active virgin CPR as a zone (BC..TC), not individual levels.
  • Inside zone — NIFTY's prior 5m close lands within [BC, TC]: every stock signal rejected, both directions.
  • Headroom — close outside the zone but within headroom × NIFTY ATR of the zone edge in the trade direction: rejected. Buys blocked when zone is overhead and close; sells blocked when zone is underfoot and close. Mirrors the 5m hurdle's directional headroom logic.
Default headroom 1.0 × ATR. Set to 0 to disable the headroom leg (the inside-zone leg stays on while the master toggle is on). Signal Trail bucket: VIRGIN_CPR_HURDLE.
Buy needs NIFTY's in-progress 1h candle green; sell needs red
Reject as wick-rejection candle when wick > × body (0 = off)
Macro-level mirror of the per-stock HTF Candle Direction Filter, applied to NIFTY's own 1h bar. When on, every stock breakout must agree with NIFTY's currently-forming 60-min candle body:
  • Buy — NIFTY 1h close > NIFTY 1h open (green). Reject if NIFTY 1h is strictly red.
  • Sell — NIFTY 1h close < NIFTY 1h open (red). Reject if NIFTY 1h is strictly green.

Wick rejection check. Even a correct-color candle is rejected if the directional wick is too long relative to the body — a "rejection candle" pattern:
  • Buy on green 1h: reject if upper wick (high − close) > ratio × body. Buyers couldn't hold the highs — selling pressure capped the move.
  • Sell on red 1h: reject if lower wick (close − low) > ratio × body. Sellers couldn't hold the lows — buying pressure floored the move.
Default ratio 2.0 — wick must be more than twice the body. Set to 0 to disable just the wick check; the color check stays on.
Doji bodies skip the wick ratio. No in-progress NIFTY 1h bar yet → fail-open. Signal Trail bucket: NIFTY_HTF_CANDLE_OPPOSED.
Per-Stock HTF Alignment Filters
Reject when prior 1h HTF candle hasn't cleared nearest weekly R1/PWH/TC/Pivot/BC (buy) or S1/PWL/TC/Pivot/BC (sell)
For a 5-min breakout, find the nearest weekly hurdle in the trade direction: buys → the highest of {R1, PWH, weekly TC, weekly Pivot, weekly BC} strictly below the 5-min close; sells → the lowest of {S1, PWL, weekly TC, weekly Pivot, weekly BC} strictly above the close. Then check the previous 1-hour HTF candle close: cleared → trade fires; didn't clear → reject; no prior 1h close yet today (first session hour) → reject. Weekly CPR (TC/Pivot/BC) is included so that breakouts that fired just past the weekly CPR boundary are gated by the prior 1h close too — e.g. a buy at R1 in a stock currently inside weekly CPR but BULLISH by the state machine. R2+/S2+ are excluded — far-out projections, not walls the HTF needs to clear first. Applies to all HPT and MPT breakouts. Fail-open when any level is warming up.
Trend: prior 1h candle close above/below 1h SMA 20 and 50 together
Pattern: 1h SMAs aligned — buy requires 20 > 50, sell requires 20 < 50
Two independent 1-hour HTF SMA checks — each rejects the trade outright on its own (no qty downgrade under the LTF-priority model).

Trend. Compares the previous completed 1h candle's close against the 1h SMA 20 and SMA 50 (both computed from completed 1h closes only — no live blending). Sticky between 1h boundaries: the gate stays put until the next 1h close commits a new value. Confirmed structure, not in-progress hope. Tue–Fri uses the prior session's final 1h close as the first-hour fallback before today's first 1h close fires; Monday morning HPT/MPT signals are rejected until the day's first 1h close.

Pattern. SMA-to-SMA ordering: 1h SMA 20 above 50 for buys, below 50 for sells. Both SMAs from completed 1h closes only. Stricter than Trend — the HTF SMA structure itself has to be stacked in the trade's direction, independent of where the prior close sits.

Applies to all HPT and MPT breakouts. Fail-open when any SMA is warming up.
Buy needs in-progress 1h candle green; sell needs red
Per-stock candle-direction confluence check. When on, every 5-min breakout must agree with the currently-forming 60-min HTF candle's body direction:
  • Buy — 1h close > 1h open (green). Reject if 1h is strictly red.
  • Sell — 1h close < 1h open (red). Reject if 1h is strictly green.
Doji (close == open) passes both directions. No in-progress 1h bar yet (first ticks of a new 1h bucket) → fail-open. Independent of HTF Hurdle / HTF SMA filters — this looks at the currently-forming bar's intra-bar direction, not the prior close. Signal Trail bucket: HTF_CANDLE_OPPOSED.
Skip any breakout with entry close beyond weekly R3 (buys) or weekly S3 (sells)
Skip any breakout with entry close beyond weekly R4 (buys) or weekly S4 (sells)
Independent of the daily extended-level skips on the Filters tab. Trades whose entry sits past the weekly extended levels carry the same exhaustion concern as a daily-R3 trade — even if the daily setup is just an R1 break. Enable both for the strictest gating; either toggle independently otherwise. Fail-open if weekly levels haven't loaded yet.
