Help & Strategy Guide
Trading strategy, setup rules, quantity management & page documentation
Trading Strategy Overview

TraderEdge is a CPR (Central Pivot Range) breakout trading bot for NSE F&O stocks. It scans for narrow and inside CPR stocks from the daily bhavcopy, monitors real-time price action on configurable timeframe candles, and enters trades when CPR levels are broken with confirming conditions.

How It Works

  • Daily Setup: Downloads NSE bhavcopy, computes CPR levels (Pivot, TC, BC, R1-R4, S1-S4, PDH, PDL) for all F&O stocks
  • Filtering: Selects stocks with narrow CPR (high breakout potential) and inside CPR (price compressed within prior day's range)
  • Z-Score Classification: Each stock's daily range is classified using 20-day history: NS Narrow + Small Range (z < -1.5) | NL Narrow + Large Range | IS Inside + Small Range | IL Inside + Large Range
  • Breakout Detection: On each candle close, checks if price broke above/below CPR levels with green/red candle confirmation
  • Entry: Market order placed, SL + Target(s) placed on fill
  • Exit: SL hit, target hit, trailing SL, or auto squareoff at configured time

Two Trading Modes

ModeDescription
Trend FollowingTrade in the direction of the breakout. CPR level breakouts + Day High/Low breakouts.
Mean ReversionTrade back toward value. Magnet trades (IV/OV days) and EV reversal trades.

Signal Source

All signals are generated by the bot's internal breakout scanner (CandleAggregator + BreakoutScanner). Real-time WebSocket ticks are aggregated into configurable timeframe candles, and breakouts are detected on each candle close. See the Architecture tab for the full signal flow diagram.

Risk Philosophy: Choppy Days Small, Trending Days Large

The core risk principle: quantity scales with conviction. Choppy/range-bound conditions get small bets that survive whipsaws. Strong trending conditions get full size to capture the move. The filter stack enforces this automatically across multiple dimensions.

The Combined Effect

On a sideways day, multiple filters compound: a counter-trend breakout inside OR with the close far from EMA might get LPT downgrade (x0.25), session move halving (x0.5), and a level factor (x0.75) - final size becomes a small fraction of base, or the trade gets skipped entirely. On a clean trending day, a weekly+daily aligned breakout with a tight candle close near EMA passes every filter at full HPT sizing.

You don't need to manually decide "is this a chop day?" The filters look at OR position, trend alignment, EMA distance, candle structure, session move, and gap classification independently - and the qty stack multiplies the results. Conviction signals get full size; weak signals get either reduced size or no trade.

20 EMA Filter

A 20-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA) is calculated on each candle close using the configured trading timeframe. It serves as two independent filters:

FilterRulePurpose
EMA Direction CheckBuy requires close > 20 EMA. Sell requires close < 20 EMA.Ensures you're trading in the direction of the short-term trend.
EMA Level Count FilterCount the CPR zones strictly between the 20 EMA and the broken level. Allow only when count = 0.Structural "stale setup" detection — EMA must sit in the zone immediately adjacent to the broken level, or inside the broken zone itself.
How the level count works

The filter iterates 9 structural zones, not individual lines. CPR, R1/PDH, and S1/PDL are each collapsed into one zone (a pair zone counts as one regardless of which edge sits in the interval):

ZoneTypeEdges
S4, S3, S2singlelevel itself
S1/PDLpairmin(S1, PL), max(S1, PL)
CPRpairmin(TC, BC), max(TC, BC)
R1/PDHpairmin(R1, PH), max(R1, PH)
R2, R3, R4singlelevel itself

Algorithm — direction-agnostic (works for both BUY and SELL):

  1. Compute broken = breakoutLevelForSetup()
  2. lo = min(ema, broken), hi = max(ema, broken)
  3. For each zone, count it if: (a) it's not the setup's own zone, AND (b) at least one of its edges is strictly in (lo, hi)
  4. Allow if count = 0, else skip

Why this works: ATR-based distance thresholds fail on wide-CPR stocks (1 ATR may span zero or multiple structural levels depending on level density). Counting zones is structural — it asks "how many S/R bands does the EMA have to cross to reach the broken level?" If any band sits between them, the setup is stale regardless of raw price distance.

Combined with Structural SL: this filter loosens the entry gate (more valid breakouts fire), and structural SL ensures the exit is anchored to the level being tested. Net effect: enter on fresh structural signals, exit on structural invalidation.

Both filters can be enabled/disabled independently. EMA is seeded from historical candles at startup so it's available from the first candle close. Shown on scanner cards in the data grid.

Target Calculation Flow

Targets go through a multi-step process before the trade is accepted:

StepWhat HappensExample (BUY)
1. CPR TargetDefault target set from CPR level (R1, R2, etc.)Entry 1850, target R2 = 1880
2. Nearest Resistance/Support ShiftIf any session high/low or weekly CPR level (R1, S1, PH, PL, TC, BC, Pivot) sits between entry and target, shift target to the nearest one. Single target — no T1/T2 split when shifted.Weekly R1=1858, session high=1865 — both between 1850-1880. Nearest is 1858 → target shifts to weekly R1
3. Small Target FilterIf final target < N x ATR from entry → skip trade (configurable, can disable)ATR=20, threshold=1.0. Distance 1858-1850=8 < 20 → SKIP

The shift picks the nearest resistance (buys) or support (sells) between entry and the structural CPR target. Candidates include today's session high/low plus all 7 weekly CPR levels. The nearest level is the realistic barrier price hits first — setting a tighter, higher-probability target.

DH/DL setups: session high/low is skipped as a candidate (the breakout candle just created it), but weekly CPR levels still apply.

When shifted: trade fires as a single full-qty OCO at the shifted target — no T1/T2 split. The shifted level is tight by design.

Magnet Trades (Mean Reversion)

Magnet trades are mean-reversion setups where price is pulled back toward the CPR (the "magnet"). Active on all day types — EV, IV, and OV.

Strategy: "Buy the dips, sell the rips." When a stock in a bullish bias dips down to S1/PDL, you buy that pullback expecting it to bounce back toward CPR. When a stock in a bearish bias rallies up to R1/PDH, you sell that rip expecting it to fade back to CPR. The CPR acts as the magnet that price gravitates toward in both directions.

SetupTriggerTargetLogic
BUY_ABOVE_S1_PDLClose above S1 and PDL (from below)CPR (min TC/BC)Buy the dip — bouncing toward CPR
SELL_BELOW_R1_PDHClose below R1 and PDH (from above)CPR (max TC/BC)Sell the rip — fading toward CPR

Quantity Rules

All magnets are classified as LPT regardless of day type. Quantity is reduced to half size (LPT factor 0.5x) because the trade is misaligned with the prevailing trend — you're fading a move rather than riding it. The strict HPT rule requires weekly + daily + breakout direction all aligned, which a counter-trend dip-buy or rip-sell can never satisfy. Half size reflects the lower conviction of trading against the dominant bias.

Guard

Close must be between S1/R1 and CPR — the target (CPR) must still be ahead, not already passed.

Probability Downgrade — IV/OV Inside-OR Rule

An otherwise HPT trade can be downgraded to LPT after initial classification if the breakout candle is still trading inside the day's opening range:

Each step multiplies the running qty from the previous step. Minimum qty = 2 (rounded to even).

Pre-Qty Filters (reject before qty calculation)

  • EV day + inside OR range — trade skipped entirely
  • EV day + OR still forming — trade skipped
  • Large candle filter — reject if candle move > N x ATR from level
  • Small candle filter — reject if candle move < N x ATR
  • Volume filter — reject if candle volume < N x average volume
  • Small target filter — reject if final target < N x ATR from entry (after nearest-level shift)
  • 20 EMA direction check — reject buy if close < 20 EMA, reject sell if close > 20 EMA (same logic as VWAP/ATP check)
  • 20 EMA level count filter — reject if any CPR zone sits strictly between the 20 EMA and the broken level (stale setup). Pair zones (CPR, R1/PDH, S1/PDL) each count once; the setup's own zone is excluded.
  • R4/S4 disabled — reject R4/S4 signals
  • ATR invalid — reject if ATR is zero or NaN
Split Targets (T1 + T2)

When enabled, every position gets two target orders: T1 at a partial distance and T2 at the full target. Each gets half the quantity.

How It Works

  • T1 price = entry + (target distance x T1%) — e.g., 50% of the way to target
  • T2 price = full target (100%)
  • T1 qty = total / 2 (rounded to even)
  • T2 qty = total - T1 qty

Fill Scenarios

EventActionTrade Record
T1 hitsCancel SL, place new SL at entry (breakeven) for remaining qtyPartial: T1 qty, reason=TARGET_1
T2 hitsCancel SL, position fully closedPartial: T2 qty, reason=TARGET_2
SL before T1Cancel both T1 and T2Full qty, reason=SL
SL after T1 (breakeven)Cancel T2Remaining qty, reason=SL_BREAKEVEN

Settings

  • Enable Split Targets — toggle on/off
  • T1 Distance — 25% / 50% / 75% of target distance
  • Min Distance — only split when target distance > N x ATR (0 = always split)
  • Requires qty ≥ 4 for split (otherwise single target)

After T1 hit, trailing SL continues from breakeven and keeps tightening.

Target Tolerance

Markets often reverse 0.10–0.50 points before touching the exact CPR level. With tolerance enabled, the bot places the target order slightly inside the level so near-miss reversals still fill the trade — converting watch-it-reverse scenarios into actual wins.

How It Works

  • Discount = ATR × targetToleranceAtr (default 0.10 → 2 points on ATR=20)
  • For buys: target order placed at structuralTarget − discount
  • For sells: target order placed at structuralTarget + discount
  • Always rounded AWAY from the level (floor for buys, ceil for sells) so the actual discount is at least the requested amount
  • Scales naturally with stock volatility — small moves on quiet stocks, larger on volatile ones

Where It Applies

Target TypeTolerance Applied?Reason
Single target trade (split disabled / qty < 4 / split distance not met)YESSits at the CPR level
T2 leg of split target tradeYEST2 is the structural CPR level
Nearest-level shifted target (session H/L or weekly CPR)YESStructural resistance/support level — single target, no split
DH/DL breakout targetYESTargets next CPR level
T1 leg of split target tradeNOT1 is a calculated mid-point, not a structural level

Example

R2 = 1880 (structural target), entry = 1860, ATR = 20, tolerance = 0.10 ATR = 2 points.

  • Without tolerance: target order at 1880. Price spikes to 1879.5, reverses, eventually hits SL. Loss recorded.
  • With tolerance: target order at 1878. Price reaches 1878 first → fills, profit booked.

Why No SL Tolerance?

Targets and SLs need opposite treatment. With targets you want to be conservative (take the win early). With SL you want to give the trade maximum benefit of the doubt — moving SL closer means tighter risk and more whipsaws. ATR-based SL placement is the right defense against stop-runs, not tolerance.

Weekly Level Reversal Detection

On the higher timeframe (default 75 min), the bot detects when price rejects at weekly R1/PWH (resistance) or S1/PWL (support). A rejection means the level held — price tested it but couldn't break through. This signals a potential mean-reversion back toward weekly CPR.

Detection (mirrors 5-min magnet pattern)

ReversalCondition (75-min candle)Meaning
WEEKLY_R1_PWH_REVERSALHigh touches R1 or PWH zone, close below min(R1,PWH), still above weekly CPR topResistance held — upside capped, price likely to retrace toward CPR
WEEKLY_S1_PWL_REVERSALLow touches S1 or PWL zone, close above max(S1,PWL), still below weekly CPR botSupport held — downside capped, price likely to bounce toward CPR

Both Path 1 (open in/above zone, close below) and Path 2 (wick through zone, close back below) are detected — same pattern as the 5-min magnet trades.

Flag Behavior

  • Flag persists across subsequent 75-min candles until nullified
  • Nullified when either:
    • A 75-min candle closes above the rejection candle's high (resistance) / below its low (support) — rejection overcome
    • Price reaches weekly CPR top (resistance) / CPR bot (support) — magnet trade complete, reverted to value
  • Re-triggered if another rejection candle appears
  • Cleared on daily reset
  • Persisted to disk for mid-day restart recovery

When weekly reversal is active for NIFTY, the weekly score component is set to 0 in the NIFTY composite score.   • Next 5-min BUY_ABOVE_R1_PDH fires → SKIPPED (buying into the weekly ceiling that just rejected)
  • Next 5-min SELL_BELOW_CPR fires → no penalty (aligned with reversal — selling toward value)
  • At 14:15, 75-min candle closes at 2645 (still below rejection high) → flag persists
  • At 15:00, 75-min candle closes at 2638 (below weekly CPR top 2640) → flag NULLIFIED (magnet complete, price reverted to CPR)
Stop Loss & Trailing SL

Initial SL — two strategies

The bot supports two SL strategies, selectable in Settings → Risk Controls:

1. Fixed SL (default) — close-based, volatility-anchored.
SL = Entry ± ATR(period) × Multiplier. Recalculated from the actual fill price when the entry fills, so the SL distance is always exactly atrMultiplier × ATR regardless of where the candle closed vs the fill.

2. Structural SL (opt-in) — level-anchored, per-setup.
When enabled, on every signal the bot computes both the Fixed SL and the structural SL (buffer default 1 ATR below/above the level the trade is reading) and picks the TIGHTER of the two — whichever sits closest to the entry. For paired zones (CPR, R1+PDH, S1+PDL) the structural anchor is the outer edge of the zone so the whole zone acts as the buffer. Day High / Day Low breakouts always use Fixed SL because a day high is just today's peak print, not a structural level to defend.

When structural SL is picked, it is NOT recalculated from fill price — the anchor is the level, not the close. The signal-time SL stays as the final SL. When Fixed SL is picked (structural was wider), the normal fill-price recalc path runs.

Result: the bot always takes the best available stop. If the structural level is nearby, structural wins (tight level-based invalidation). If the structural level is too far, default volatility-based SL wins (avoids over-wide stops). No max-distance setting is needed — the Fixed SL serves as the natural cap.

Why paired levels anchor to the outer edge: between R1 and PDH, the price is "inside the zone" — neither breakout nor invalidation. A stop at max − buffer gets shaken out by retests into the zone. Anchoring to min − buffer lets the entire zone act as the structural buffer before the stop hits.

Structural SL ↔ EMA Level Count Filter Synergy

The two filters compose into a clean structural strategy:

  • EMA level count filter loosens the entry gate — take every setup where the EMA is in the zone immediately adjacent to the broken level (or inside the broken zone itself). More valid breakouts fire, especially on wide-CPR stocks where ATR-based thresholds used to kill them.
  • Structural SL anchors the exit to the level being tested — if the breakout fails and price returns through the level, the SL triggers at the real structural invalidation point, not at an arbitrary ATR offset.

Net effect: enter on fresh structural signals, exit on structural invalidation. "Right by structure, wrong by structure" — the entire premise of level-based trading.

Side benefits:

  • Better R/R on tight breakouts — close near the level means tighter structural SL, same target distance, higher R multiple
  • Natural fail-fast on false breakouts — if the level doesn't hold, SL hits quickly (small loss), trade doesn't bleed through ATR decay
  • Fewer morning rejections on gap days — EMA catching up is fine as long as no CPR zone sits between it and the broken level

Tradeoff: more trades fire → daily risk budget (maxDailyLoss) consumes faster if early trades lose. Watch for "risk exposure limit reached" warnings and adjust maxDailyLoss or tighten the structural SL buffer if needed.

Trailing SL (ATR Peak-Based)

  • Tracks peak price (LONG) or trough price (SHORT) since entry
  • New SL = peak - ATR x trailing multiplier (LONG) or trough + ATR x trailing multiplier (SHORT)
  • Activation threshold: trailing only starts after price moves N x ATR in profit (default 1.0 ATR)
  • SL only tightens — never loosens (LONG: SL moves up, SHORT: SL moves down)
  • Modified on Fyers via order modification API (not cancel/replace)

Auto Square Off

All open positions squared off at configured time (e.g., 15:10). SL and target orders cancelled, market exit orders placed.

Key Timing Rules

  • Before trading start: signals are rejected ("outside trading hours")
  • Before first candle close: open classification is unknown, OR is "FORMING". Non-EV trades proceed normally. EV detection not yet possible.

Order Management & Trade Lifecycle

1. Signal to Order

When the breakout scanner detects a valid signal, it passes through SignalProcessor for validation and quantity calculation. If approved:

  • A market order (MPP — Market Protection Price) is placed via Fyers API
  • Order type: 2 (Market) | Side: 1 (BUY) or -1 (SELL) | Product: INTRADAY
  • The entry order ID is registered for fill monitoring

2. Entry Fill Detection

Two parallel paths monitor the entry order:

PathHowSpeedWhen Active
WebSocket (OrderEventService)Fyers Order WebSocket pushes order status changes in real-timeInstant (~100ms)When Order WS is connected
Polling (PollingService)Polls Fyers order book every 2 seconds checking order status2-10 secondsFallback when WS is down

On fill detection (status = 2 / Traded):

  • Actual fill price (tradedPrice) extracted from order book — never uses Fyers blended netAvgPrice
  • Fixed SL: recalculated from actual fill price: SL = fillPrice ± ATR × multiplier
  • Structural SL (opt-in): kept at signal-time value — anchor is the S/R level, not the fill price
  • Position state saved to database (symbol, side, qty, entry, SL, target)
  • PositionManager updated (LONG/SHORT/NONE tracker)
  • Market data subscriptions updated to include new symbol

3. SL + Target Placement (OCO)

Immediately after entry fill, SL and target orders are placed:

OrderTypeQtyPrice
Stop LossType 3 (Stop-Market)Full qtyFixed: Entry ± ATR × multiplier. Structural: level ± buffer × ATR (per setup). Rounded to tick.
Target (single)Type 1 (Limit)Full qtyComputed target price (rounded to tick)
T1 (split)Type 1 (Limit)Half qtyEntry + N% of target distance
T2 (split)Type 1 (Limit)Half qtyFull target price
  • Retry logic: up to 3 attempts. If one order succeeds and the other fails, the successful one is cancelled and both retried together.
  • Fallback: if all retries fail, scanForManualOCO() scans the order book for manually placed SL/target orders matching the symbol.
  • Order IDs saved to position state for tracking and restart recovery.

4. OCO Monitoring (One-Cancels-Other)

Once SL + Target are placed, they are monitored as an OCO pair — when one fills, the other is cancelled.

PathHowWhat It Detects
WebSocketOrder status push events from FyersFill (status 2), cancellation (status 1), rejection (status 5), modification (price change on status 6)
PollingPolls order book every 5 secondsSame statuses, plus detects manual price modifications

5. Fyers Order Status Codes

CodeMeaningBot Action
1CancelledOrder was cancelled (by bot, user, or exchange). If SL cancelled without fill, position may be unprotected.
2Traded (Filled)Order executed. For entry: place SL+Target. For SL/Target: cancel counterpart, record trade.
5RejectedOrder rejected by exchange. Logged as error. Entry rejection stops the trade. SL rejection = unprotected position.
6Pending (Open)Order is live, waiting to fill. Bot monitors for status change. Detects price modifications.

6. OCO Fill Handling

Single Target Mode:

EventAction
SL fillsCancel target order. Record trade (reason: SL or TRAILING_SL). Clear position state.
Target fillsCancel SL order. Record trade (reason: TARGET). Clear position state.

Split Target Mode (T1 + T2):

EventAction
T1 fillsCancel current SL (full qty). Place new SL at breakeven (entry price) for remaining qty. Record partial trade (TARGET_1). Update position state.
T2 fillsCancel SL. Record partial trade (TARGET_2). Clear position state.
SL fills (before T1)Cancel both T1 and T2. Record full trade (SL). Clear position state.
SL fills (after T1)Cancel T2. Record remaining qty trade (SL_BREAKEVEN). Clear position state.

7. Trailing SL Modification

  • MarketDataService monitors LTP on every WebSocket tick
  • Tracks peak price (LONG) or trough price (SHORT) since entry
  • When profit exceeds activation threshold (N × ATR), trailing begins
  • New SL = peak - ATR × trailing multiplier (only tightens, never loosens)
  • Fyers API: PUT /api/v3/orders/sync modifies the SL order price in-place (no cancel/replace)
  • Updated SL price saved to position state and tracked in OCO context

8. Manual Modifications

If you manually modify SL or target price on Fyers terminal:

  • Both WebSocket and polling paths detect price changes on pending orders (status 6)
  • New prices are synced back to position state (saveOcoState())
  • OCO context updated so cancellation uses correct counterpart
  • Trailing SL respects manual SL — it only tightens from the current price, never loosens it

9. Position Sync (Safety Net)

Every 10 seconds, syncPosition() compares broker positions with bot state:

  • Detects new positions opened outside the bot (manual trades)
  • Detects positions closed externally (manual squareoff)
  • Resolves entry price: in-memory → disk → order book lookup → Fyers avg (last resort)
  • Auto-scans for SL/target orders on newly detected positions
  • Skipped entirely when both WebSockets are connected (zero API polling)

10. Square Off Process

Triggered manually (button) or automatically at configured time:

  • For each open position: cancel all pending SL/target orders first
  • Place market exit order (opposite side of position)
  • Wait for exit fill, record trade with reason "AUTO_SQUAREOFF" or "MANUAL"
  • Clear all position state
  • Telegram notification sent

11. Crash Recovery

On restart, the bot recovers state from the database:

  • Position state (symbol, side, qty, entry, SL, target, order IDs) loaded from SQLite
  • OR state (opening range, first candle close, day open) loaded from JSON file
  • Scanner state (broken levels, signal history) loaded from JSON file
  • Active positions re-detected via syncPosition()
  • OCO monitoring resumed for positions with saved SL/target order IDs
  • Split target state (T1 filled, remaining qty) preserved

Order Flow Diagram

SIGNAL → SignalProcessor (validate, qty, target) → MARKET ORDER placed → WAIT FOR FILL (WS push or 2s poll) → FILLED (extract tradedPrice) → Recalculate SL from fill price → PLACE SL + TARGET (retry x3) → MONITOR OCO (WS push or 5s poll) → SL/TARGET FILLS → Cancel counterpart → Record trade → Clear state

Trend, Alignment & Probability

Dual Timeframes for Trend

The bot uses two independent timeframes to determine trend direction. Both trends are candle-close-based (not live LTP), updated once per candle close and stable between closes.

TrendCandle TimeframePrice SourceWhat It Tells You
Daily TrendTrading TF (default 5 min)Last 5-min candle close vs today's daily CPR levelsToday's intraday bias — is price above or below today's value area?
Weekly TrendHigher TF (default 75 min)Last 75-min candle close vs weekly CPR levelsThe bigger picture — is the stock in a multi-day uptrend, downtrend, or range?

Why candle-close-based? Live LTP can flip trends tick-by-tick during a candle. Candle-close-based trends update once per interval and remain stable — the BreakoutScanner always evaluates probability against a consistent trend, not a flickering one.

First candle fallback: Before the first candle closes on each timeframe (5 min for daily, 75 min for weekly), the trend falls back to live LTP vs CPR levels. Once the first candle closes, trends lock to the closed price. On mid-day restart, persisted candle-close data is restored so trends resume immediately.

Trend Alignment & Probability

Probability rules differ by setup type:

Trend Breakouts (CPR, R1-R4, S1-S4, DH/DL)

HPT requires weekly + daily + breakout all aligned. Daily check ensures the stock is trending in the breakout direction today, not just on the weekly timeframe.

WeeklyDailyBreakoutProbabilityWhy
BullishBullishBuyHPTAll three aligned — highest conviction
BearishBearishSellHPTAll three aligned — highest conviction
NeutralAnyEitherLPTNo weekly bias — strict HPT rule not met
BullishBearishBuyLPTWeekly supports but daily opposes — mixed signals
BearishAnyBuyLPTBuying against weekly downtrend
BullishAnySellLPTSelling against weekly uptrend

Magnet Trades & EV Reversals — Always LPT

Magnet trades (BUY_ABOVE_S1_PDL, SELL_BELOW_R1_PDH) and EV reversal trades (mean-reversion on gap days) are counter-daily by design — you're buying a pullback or selling a bounce. They can never satisfy the strict HPT rule (weekly+daily+direction aligned), so they're always classified as LPT with the configured LPT qty factor (default 0.5x).

This is intentional. The HPT/LPT split is binary: either everything is aligned (HPT, full size) or it's not (LPT, half size). Magnet and reversal setups are valid trades but inherently weaker conviction — appropriate for half size, not full.

Why Weekly Trend Matters More Than Daily

  • Weekly trend reflects the structural bias over 5 trading days — institutional flows, sector rotation, multi-day momentum
  • Daily trend is just where price is right now vs today's pivot — it can flip multiple times in a day
  • A stock can be daily bearish (price below today's CPR) but weekly bullish (above weekly CPR) — this means today is a pullback in an uptrend, which is actually a buying opportunity
  • The reverse is also true: daily bullish but weekly bearish = a bounce in a downtrend, selling opportunity

Risk Management

Risk Budget

The bot enforces a daily risk budget to prevent catastrophic losses.

ParameterFormulaExample
Max Daily LossTotal Capital x Max Risk Per Day %₹5,00,000 x 2% = ₹10,000
Exposed RiskSum of (entry - SL) x qty for all open positions3 positions = ₹2,000 + ₹1,500 + ₹1,800 = ₹5,300
Consumed RiskRealized losses from closed trades today2 losing trades = ₹1,200 + ₹800 = ₹2,000
Total RiskExposed + Consumed₹5,300 + ₹2,000 = ₹7,300
Remaining BudgetMax Daily Loss - Total Risk₹10,000 - ₹7,300 = ₹2,700 remaining

When total risk reaches the daily limit, new signals are rejected ("risk exposure limit reached"). Existing positions continue to be managed.

Risk Per Trade

Each trade's risk is capped at a fixed rupee amount:

  • Base qty = Risk Per Trade / SL Distance
  • Example: Risk ₹500, SL distance ₹20 → qty = 25
  • This ensures no single trade can lose more than the configured amount (before slippage)

Capital Per Trade

A secondary cap on position size:

  • Capital cap qty = (Capital Per Trade x Leverage) / LTP
  • Final qty = min(risk qty, capital cap qty)
  • Prevents outsized positions on low-price stocks where risk-based qty might be very large

Exposed Risk Updates

Exposed risk is recalculated in real-time as SL prices change:

  • Trailing SL tightens → exposed risk decreases
  • SL moves above entry (breakeven) → exposed risk = 0 for that position
  • T1 hit → SL at breakeven, remaining position is risk-free
  • Displayed on Home dashboard and Positions page hero cards

Safety Guards

  • Kill switch — admin toggle on Positions page. When OFF, no new trades accepted. Existing positions still managed.
  • Dedup guard — 5-second window prevents duplicate trade recordings from race conditions
  • Min qty = 2 — positions always have at least 2 shares (allows even split for T1/T2)
  • Tick rounding — all prices rounded to exchange tick size (from Fyers symbol master)
  • Crash recovery — position state persisted to SQLite, OCO order IDs survive restarts
